Tug of War

The Simulation:

Tug Of War is a simple game that most people are aquainted with. To win a robot needs to push the box to the otherside of the stage and it is possible to end in a draw

Teaching notes:

Tug of War serves as the starting point to perfectly introduce the coding and physics to students. If coded well, most competing robots will draw

Drag Race

The Simulation:

Drag Race is a also quite simple. The first robot to reach the end wins

Teaching notes:

While at first the tactic seems simple, move at max speed. This allows students to play around with different tactics (e.g. barging into the enemy) in a contaned enviroment


Robo Sumo

The Simulation:

Robo Sumo is, as the name hints, a robotic version of sumo wrestling. A robot wins by causing the other to fall off the stage.

Teaching notes:

Robo Sumo is a great introduction to both more complex strategies and sensor programming. For a robot to have a good chance of wining, it would have to detect the location of the the other using its distance sensors.

Cage Fight

The Simulation:

Cage Fight is similar to Robo Sumo exept the Robot that ends up in on of the four death zones looses.

Teaching notes:

While the base tactic is similar to Robo Sumo, there is much posibility to experiment with more complex strategies as there is a specific location the Robot is tying to avoid.
